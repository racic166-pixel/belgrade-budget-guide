3 Days in Belgrade 🇷🇸

This itinerary focuses on practical and enjoyable ways to experience Belgrade without treating the city like a checklist. It combines central attractions, cafés, riverside areas, and slower local neighborhoods.

Day 1 — Historic Center

Knez Mihailova & Kalemegdan

Start in the pedestrian center, walk toward Kalemegdan Fortress, and spend time near the river viewpoints.

In the evening, Skadarlija is a good area for traditional Serbian food and live music.

Day 2 — Zemun & Riverside Areas

Zemun Walk

Zemun has a slower atmosphere than the city center and is known for riverside cafés, walking paths, and older architecture.

It’s one of the better places to spend a relaxed afternoon.

Day 3 — Dorćol & Ada Ciganlija

Cafés & Relaxed Areas

Dorćol is known for cafés and quieter streets, while Ada Ciganlija is popular for outdoor activities and walking near the water.

Budget Notes

Belgrade can still be more affordable than many European capitals, especially when using local bakeries, public transport, and accommodation outside premium tourist zones.
