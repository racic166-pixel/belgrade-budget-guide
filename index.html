Belgrade Travel Guide 🇷🇸

A real, honest guide to exploring Belgrade — from hidden streets and local food to sunset spots and budget travel tips.

Welcome to Belgrade

Belgrade is not a polished European capital — and that’s exactly why people fall in love with it. It’s loud, chaotic, historic, and full of life at every hour of the day. Walk through the city and you’ll see brutalist buildings next to cafés filled with locals drinking coffee for hours. It’s a place where old meets new in a very raw, authentic way.

Best Places to Visit

Kalemegdan Fortress

This is where Belgrade breathes. Locals come here at sunset, not just tourists. You’ll see river views, street musicians, and couples sitting on the old stone walls.

Knez Mihailova Street

The main pedestrian street. It’s always alive — cafés, shops, street performers. Best experienced early morning or late evening when the crowds calm down.

Food & Local Culture

Serbian food is simple, heavy, and extremely satisfying. You’ll see grilled meat everywhere — ćevapi, pljeskavica, and burek are everyday food, not “tourist dishes”. The best meals are not in fancy restaurants, but in small grill shops where locals eat lunch daily.

Budget Tips

Belgrade is one of the most affordable capitals in Europe. You don’t need a big budget to enjoy the city — walking is the best way to explore, and most experiences are based on atmosphere, not money.

What It Feels Like

Belgrade is not about ticking off attractions — it’s about wandering. You’ll find yourself sitting in cafés for hours, walking without a plan, and discovering places that aren’t on any list. That’s the real experience of the city.
